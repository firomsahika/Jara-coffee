Our History

From Grandmother's Gift to Global Legacy: The Story of Kerchanshe Coffee and Bale Mountain Farm

1990

The Beginning

Kerchanshe Coffee’s journey began with the passion of our CEO’s grandfather, whose deep connection to the land and love for coffee farming took root in the fertile soils of southern Ethiopia. His family's coffee-growing tradition originated with Mr. Israel Degefa's grandmother, Mrs. Wagayehu Bekele Debela, who instilled sustainable farming values at Bale Mountain Farm.

Expanding

Building on this strong foundation, Mr. Israel Degefa transformed the family legacy into Kerchanshe Coffee—now Ethiopia’s largest coffee producer and exporter. Over the past 15 years, Kerchanshe Coffee has grown from humble beginnings into a global leader in the coffee industry, employing over 1,900 permanent staff and 25,000 seasonal workers. The company’s operations directly and indirectly impact the livelihoods of more than 1 million coffee growers across southern and southwestern Ethiopia, staying true to its heritage of quality and sustainability.

Leading Coffee Expert

Bale Mountain Farm remains a shining example of the commitment to tradition and innovation that defines Kerchanshe Coffee. Under Mr. Israel Degefa’s stewardship, the farm has seen significant improvements, including the implementation of drip irrigation systems and the use of high-quality, chemical-free coffee seedlings. These advancements ensure the production of the purest coffee beans while honoring the traditional cultivation methods passed down by his grandmother.

Community

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

Kerchanshe believes in being socially responsible. We believe in giving back, and contributing to the community that we live in. That's engraved in our DNA. We engage with the community through our NGO, Buna Qela.

Since its establishment, Buna Qela has implemented a number of social development and coffee improvement projects. The main ones include social development projects like clean water supply, electric supply, construction of access road, construction of primary schools, construction clinics (human and veterinary), construction of farmers training center and construction of church; and coffee improvement activities like distribution of quality coffee seedlings, stumping, pruning, compost and training on quality and requirements of different certification standards.
